Scenario: Reviewing a SAFE from an angel investor

VentureCounsel.AI Output:

Risk Flag: Missing pro-rata rights. This SAFE converts without giving you a seat at the next raise.

Market Check: Angels writing $50K+ typically expect pro-rata for at least one follow-on round.

Action: Add to Section 2(b): "Investor shall have the right to participate in the Company's next equity financing up to Investor's pro-rata share based on the conversion of this instrument."

↑ Specific, actionable, references market norms

Scenario: Drafting founder vesting terms

VentureCounsel.AI Output:

Standard Structure: 4-year vest, 1-year cliff, monthly thereafter.

Hidden Issue: You need double-trigger acceleration for M&A. Without it, acquirer can fire you day 1 and you lose unvested equity.

Language to Add:

"Upon a Change of Control, if Founder's service is terminated without Cause within 12 months following such transaction, 50% of then-unvested shares shall immediately vest."

↑ Anticipates what you didn't know to ask

Common questions

Is this actually legal advice?

No. VentureCounsel.AI is a tool that helps you draft and understand contracts. It doesn't create an attorney-client relationship. For high-stakes deals, you should still have a real lawyer review. But for 80% of early-stage work, this gives you production-ready drafts that save you thousands in legal fees.

Do I need to know legal terminology?

Nope. Just describe your situation in plain English: "I'm raising $500K on a SAFE and the investor sent me this term sheet" or "I need a founder vesting agreement for 3 co-founders." It will guide you from there.

How is this different from ChatGPT?

Regular ChatGPT explains concepts and gives generic examples.
